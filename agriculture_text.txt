Agriculture is the practice of cultivating soil, growing crops, and raising livestock for food and other products.

Wheat is one of the most important cereal crops in the world. Wheat grows best in well drained loamy soil and requires moderate temperatures.

Rice is a staple food for more than half of the world's population. Rice grows best in flooded fields and warm climates.

Maize, also known as corn, requires fertile soil and adequate sunlight. Nitrogen fertilizers are often used to increase maize yield.

Cotton is an important fiber crop. Cotton plants require warm temperatures and moderate rainfall for optimal growth.

Tomato plants can suffer from early blight disease. Early blight causes brown spots on leaves and can reduce crop yield.

Potato plants require cool temperatures and well drained soil. Potato crops may suffer from late blight disease caused by fungal infection.

Soybean crops need well drained soil and proper irrigation. Soybeans are rich in protein and oil.

Fertilizers supply nutrients to crops. The three main nutrients are nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.

Organic farming uses natural fertilizers like compost and manure instead of synthetic chemicals.

Pesticides are used to control pests that damage crops. However excessive pesticide use can harm the environment.

Crop rotation helps maintain soil fertility and reduce pest problems.

Irrigation is the artificial application of water to soil to help crops grow when rainfall is insufficient.

Soil health is important for sustainable agriculture. Healthy soil contains organic matter, microorganisms, and nutrients.

Farmers must monitor weather conditions such as rainfall, temperature, and humidity to manage crops effectively.

Sustainable agriculture focuses on environmentally friendly farming practices that maintain productivity for future generations.
